Hi, I'm Sarita.
I find the story
in complex data.

I'm a biostatistician and data scientist with a deep interest in how data can improve human and environmental health. I recently completed my M.S. in Biostatistics at Georgetown University, where my research focused on cognitive outcomes in breast cancer survivors and evaluating machine learning methods for handling missing clinical data.

My background in Biotechnology and Environmental Health gives me a unique lens. I think about health problems at every scale, from a single patient's experience to contaminants in an entire watershed.

I've worked across some pretty different environments: an environmental lab with Fairfax County, a global health consulting project gathering patient insights across multiple countries, and academic research diving deep into longitudinal clinical datasets. What ties it all together is a genuine curiosity about how data, when communicated well, can drive better decisions. I care just as much about a clean, intuitive visualization as I do about the analysis behind it.

Outside of work, I stay creative and grounded. You'll find me sewing or crocheting, out on a hike with my dog, or buried in a good book.

I'm currently looking for opportunities in statistics, public health, academic research, or data science where I can keep doing work that matters. If that sounds like a fit, I'd love to connect!
